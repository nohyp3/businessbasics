Basics

So... what even is business?

According to investopedia, a business is defined as "an organization or enterprise entity engaged in commercial, industrial, or professional activities". Businesses can be for profit or non-profit. Business can also refer to the giving of goods/services for money.

Now what is an economy?

An economy is the connection of production, consumption and exchange activities in how scarce resources are allocated. Everyone plays a part in an economy because an economy encompasses or covers all activity related to the production, consumption and trade of goods and services.

There are 3 types of economies:

  1. Command Economies - Where the government controls the economy. There is a lack of freedom in this type of economy and there are no property or intellectual property rights.
  2. Free Economies - Are widely embraced and used almost everywhere. There is complete freedom, little to no government involvement and promotes innovation. Note: There is no pure or truely free economy
  3. Mixed Economies - Slightly free or command market leaning

Why businesses matter:

When businesses succeed, society also wins. More jobs ar created, more money and more taxes are produced.

What are regulations?

Regulations are meant to protect people, the environment, companies and more. It can be argued that too many regulations may make it difficult for entreprenures & businesses to compete or even start. An example is Canada's dairy market which is heavily regulated to ensure quality.

What are monopolies, duopolies, trioplogies and oligopolies?

A Monopoly is when a single entity has complete control over an industry. A duopoly is where there are two entities that have complete control over an industry and a triopoly is where there are three. An oliogopoly is when a handful of companies have complete control over an industry

Forms of Business Ownership

Sole Proprietorship - Owned by a single person who is the proprietor, the owner has the sole responsibility. The owner has unlimited liability, meaning that they have the risk of losing personal assets if the business fails. The set up and administration is easierand less expensive.

Partnerships - Operated by two people or more, same as a sole proprietorship. General partnerships mean that both owners are liable and equal as partners. Limited partnerships have a general partner who manages the partnership, associates are the limited partners who supply capital, but their liability is limited to their investment.

Corporations - A business granted legal status with rights, privileges and liabilities distinct from the people who work for it. They can be as small as 1 person with 3 board of directions or as large as multinationals. Owners are shareholders, management team runs companies and board, they are responsible for ensuring the management acts in the best interest of shareholders.

  • Private - shares are not listed to the public and only few control them
  • Public - raise money by making shares
  • Crown - owned by the government
  • Cooperative - owned by workers or members that buy the product
  • Franchise - licenses the rights to name, procedure, designs. They are owned by the franchisee (person who opened the franchise)
