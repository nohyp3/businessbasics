Corporate Social Responsibility

What are ethics?

Ethics can be described as a code of moral principals (standards for good or bad, right or wrong).

Ethical behavior - What is accepted as good and right in the context of the governing moral code

How can companies respect core or universal values? They can have respect for human dignity, create a valuing culture, ensure safety is a priority and much more!

What are some possible issues in the workplace?

There could be ethical dilemmas, choices that benefit owners/the business but may be unethical.

An ethical dilemma may be related to discrimination, harassment, conflict of interest, customer confidence, organizational resources

Ethical Leadership - Managers are role models who influence behaviour

CSR - A self regulating business

The classic view is that management maximizes profits. The socio economic view is concerned for broader social welfare, not just profits. The modern view is that strong purpose means profitability and longevity.
